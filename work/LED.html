LEDs around Emory Implementing Sustainable Lighting (and the future)

A deep fascination for LEDs brought me to take action in areas at Emory University where sustainabile lighting might make campus (and the future) seem a bit brighter.



I. All of the Lights

I like LEDs a lot. I find the tiny semi-conductors so cool because they're an elegant solution for sustainability challenges. They embody the fusion of technology with a futuristic, minimalist design, which is the core of who I am. My roommate and I bought a pair of multicolored LED string lights a couple of years ago because we wanted to make our dorm room look like it was straight out of Tron. However, I soon found many opportunities to use my interest in LEDs and sustainable technology with my desire to implement change on my campus.

II. The Patio Problem

I noticed that the patio area outside my residence hall wasn't being used after dark. People would study and socialize there during the day, but once the sun set, it became deserted and underused. I saw this as an opportunity to:

  1. Make a positive change on campus
  2. Test some design thinking skills
  3. Play with LEDs
I applied for an incentives fund grant from the Office of Sustainability Iniatives and installed new LED lights outside, paired with some LEDs inside. The interior lights replaced old T8 CFLs, which earned the building some energy savings, meaning the additional lights outside wouldn't drive the energy consumption of the building any higher.

Explore my grant application on your own.

III. Lighting the Way

The project was an experiment in modifying people's behavior and attempting to promote the sustainable lighting. Although I haven't noticed a change in the number of people using the space, collaborating with Campus Services and Housing Operations taught me a lot about campus projects. Most notably, it revealed to me the need for education and outreach. Many of the employees I worked with knew the benefits of LED lighting, but lacked the push by administrators or students (or the funding) to do anything.

Seeking change, I used the connections I forged to continue fighting to replace CFLs installed in the vast majority of fixtures on campus, including presenting a cost-benefit analysis to the Director of Facilities and Operations. In addition, I've worked with other students to submit their own grants and requests for sustainable lighting, including Greeks Go Green's proposal for LED light in Greek Housing.

With these projects and pursuits, I hoped to contribute to Emory's Sustainability Vision and goals of reducing energy consumption while spreading the aesthetic that I've come to cherish.

TL;DR:

  • Applied for and obtained a $1,400 grant
  • Collaborated with Director of Housing Operations and campus engineers to install lights
  • Presented a plan to Director of Facilities and Operations to install additional LEDs in Residence Halls
