Emory Climate Organization Climate Activism at Emory

Emory Climate Organization,

or ECO for short, is a chartered organization at Emory University that seeks to augment climate literacy and activism on campus. However, the organization initially started as a project in my Paris is an Explanation class. This Interdisclinary Studies (IDS) class was a joint collaboration with the Goizueta Business School and Emory College's Environmental Science and Journalism departments to study, and attend, the climate change negotiations occuring at COP21 in Paris, France.

During the semester, our multidisciplinary cohort made podcasts about sustainability issues at Emory, wrote blog posts about COP21 on our website (that I created and managed), and ran a model UN simulation of the negotiations as we geared up for the conference in November 2015.

Climate Week

was our way of bringing our experiences at COP21 back to our campus. We hosted an art gallery of climate change related art, debates sponsored by The Carter Center, environmental justice dialogues, but it was really just the beginning of our efforts; our class coalesced to pressure university administration on divestment from fossil fuels. I was in charge of the marketing and branding team, which went hand in hand with my charge to create a recognizable institution. Since 90% of us were graduating seniors, everything we learned in our class and implemented during Climate Week would mean nothing if we didn't find a way to keep our work going. It only made sense to create infrastructure that would carry the torch of climate action and education past our time at Emory.

I wrote, along with two other classmates, a mission and draft constitution for Emory Climate Organization, whose charter was officially recognized by the Student Government Association in Fall 2016.

All of the SGA members who approved the charter had the ECO logo sticker that I co-designed on their laptops.
