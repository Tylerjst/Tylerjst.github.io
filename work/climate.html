UN Climate Delegation Fundraiser

Developing marketing materials for Emory's Climate Delegation

Emory University's UN Climate Delegation needed funding to stay alive. My team initiated a fundraising effort, but we needed a marketing strategy to garner interest. I made educational videos, logos, and other materials to support the organization.

I. Overview

The Challenge

Emory's UN Climate Delegation sent student delegates to two UNFCCC conferences in Paris and Marrakech in 2015 and 2016; however, their funding sources dwindled in early 2017. This funding was essential since most students, including myself, relied on financial aid to attend the conferences. In order to preserve the delegation and give future students the opportunity I was fortunate enough to receive, I helped organize a fundraiser with my old professor and one of my co-delegates.

Fundraising money and establishing a list of donors would garner long-term support for the Delegation. If we reached our target of $3,000, we would have a better argument to justify the delegation's existence and increase the odds of receiving university funding in the future. Visit the fundraising site here.

However, we needed a way to spread awareness about our campaign.

  1. How would we promote the fundraiser?
  2. How would we explain what COP and climate action is?
  3. How would we encourage people to donate?



Our Solution

Our marketing strategy consisted of three parts:

  1. 1 long(ish) educational video about Climate Change and COP.
  2. 6 short, promotional videos about COP to be used in email blasts.
  3. A limited edition sticker (with Thank You Notes) for the first 100 people to donate.

By the end of the fundraiser, we raised $3,220, surpassing our goal of $3,000. This also ensured an ongoing partnership with the Office of Sustainability Initiatives, continuing the delegation for at least another year.

II. The Process

1 Long(ish) Educational Video

I made the main video as a general introduction of climate change and how COP (as well as Emory's Delegation) seeks to address it. By watching this, one would hopefully understand that climate is an interdisciplinary problem and that everyone has a stake in trying to solve it. In addition, the main video also showcased footage from COP22 to give a sense to donors what the conference is like.

6 Short Promotional Videos

This was the same purpose as the 6 shorter videos. I used the footage I shot from COP22 video to create a video series of 6, 30 second shorts to be used in email blasts. These showcased snippets from the conference to portray the experience to potential donors.

A Limited Edition Sticker (and Thank You Notes)

Furthermore, I came up with the idea of creating a limited edition sticker to be sent to donors at the conclusion of the fundraiser. This gift, borrowing some structure from kickstarter campaigns, would hopefuly entice some people to donate even a small bit of money.


The final thank you letters we sent out to all donors were printed on recycled paper and featured a new logo for Emory's UN Climate Delegation that I designed.




Our fundraising campaign was hosted on a momentum webpage run by Emory University. All of our videos were sent via email blasts, but could also be found on the fundraiser homepage.

III. Concluding thoughts

By the end of the fundraiser, our team raised $3,220, surpassing our goal of $3,000, solidifying a partnership with the Office of Sustainability Initiatives, and ensuring at least one more year of Emory's Climate Delegation.
