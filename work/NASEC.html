NASEC 2014 A Naval Academy Executive Report

Don't give up the ship

I served as the scribe for a team of engineering students that was tasked with developing an executive report detailing an idea that would incorporate machine learning concepts covered at the conference. Admittedly, machine learning and automation is not my area of expertise, but I recognized my strength at communicating futuristic visions, using that to set the tone of the language in our report and to propose the idea that our group ultimately chose: an automated swarm of extraterrestrial rovers called Extraterrestrial Vehicular Explorer, or EVE. Yes, the name was totally inspired by WALL-E.

Explore the report on your own

The problem

that EVE seeks to solve regards data. It's not usual , but when a rover streams raw data from another plant back to Earth, the speed of light suddenly becomes a dial-up modem from the 90s. Scientists back on earth must wait long amounts of time to retrieve this information before being able do anything with it, hindering the amount of science they can science. However, this process can be streamlined by developing a machine learning algorithim that utilize a swarm of rovers to scan for data, sort it, identify "flagged" information that would be of interest to scientists, then send those filtered results to earth, shrinking the amount of data and cutting the amount of time needed to obtain mission results.

There was a lot to unpack with this idea, such as the philosophical debate of whether or not robots can perform science, but those deliberations honestly made the writing process way more fun.
