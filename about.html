I am Tyler Stern.

I'm the kind of person who used to pull VCR tapes apart when I was younger to figure out how they worked. Chewing on problems is fun, but playing with a bunch of smart and creative people to find solutions brings me an unmatched amount of joy. In 2016, I graduated from Emory University with a B.S. in Applied Physics and spent time exploring Environmental Science, Theater, Creative Writing, and Film.

I'm currently a Master's Student at Carnegie Mellon University studying Human-Computer Interaction and focusing on how to integrate technology and design into civic infrastructure. I believe public services should foster sustainability, equity, and wonder about the universe.


I'm also the kind of person who watches an ungodly amount of YouTube videos and makes silly noises with my mouth. Society tends to call this "beatboxing" and is now mistaking it for talent.
