Writing

StoriesHouse

started off as a blog that my high-school friend made so the rest of our friends could share stories from college and stay in touch. When we reunited the following summer, we rekindled our friendship with a newly discovered passion for writing and media, and decided StoriesHouse.com was going to be our laboratory. We began posting our writing projects here as we took a hands-on approach to learning how to write different pieces of fiction and non-fiction, as well as how to manage a media platform. It allowed us to test different mediums, including podcasts and videos. I used the site to post some satirical articles and short stories that I wrote for classes and in my free time.

The Emory Spoke

is Emory University's satirical magazine that publishes all sorts of garbage. I was excited to join the group so that perhaps my poop jokes could be developed into something a bit more refined. Turns out they really like that kind of stuff. I also did some silly photoshop work for their bi-annual magazines. The magazine gave me an opportunity to work on my writing voice and sharpen the satirical and sarcastic tone that I've come to appreciate in great comedy writing.


You can read some of the articles I wrote, but they are all published anonymously, so....you're just going to have to trust that I'm this bizzare.
